Does it matter how many elements my soil test looked at?

The more detailed your soil test the better! Try to find a lab that can provide you with a detailed test, that will include testing of all micro nutrients.

If you have test results of only some of the elements, you can still use SMART Lite. But keep in mind, the goal is to optimize your fertilization. The more input we have on the current condition of your field, the more precise a fertilizer plan SMART Lite can provide you with.

What are "Extraction Methods" and why they are important?

When going through the SMART Lite wizard, you will be asked to enter soil test results together with the extraction methods used. It is important that you enter them correctly as found on the soil test results you have.

The extraction method is the method used to extract a specific nutrient from the soil sample. Every extraction method is relevant for a specific soil character and to get a good estimation of the availability of the nutrient.

For example: Olsen/Bray 1 is a common method used for Phosphorus, Ammonium Acetate/ Mehlich 3 for Potassium, DTPA for micro elements, etc.

For more information, read our article Interpreting Soil Test Results - The Extraction Method.

 Is SMART Lite for hydroponic growers?

Currently SMART Lite only delivers fertilization plans for soil-grown crops. Please consider purchasing our SMART Premium software, which fully supports hydroponics.

I am using fertigation – can I use SMART Lite?

Yes. SMART Lite fertilization plans are suitable for fertigation. But bear in mind, that you will have to calculate your solution on your own from the plan’s details.
